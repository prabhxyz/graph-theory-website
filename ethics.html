Ethical Issues in Graph Theory

Even though graph theory is just math, its uses can raise ethical questions. Here are some key points in simple words:

Graphs can show personal connections. It is important to get people’s permission and protect their private data.

Graphs can be used to watch people. This may harm civil rights if done without proper rules.

Graph algorithms might favor some groups over others. It is important to test and fix these biases.

It is important that systems using graphs explain how they work. People should know how decisions are made.

When studying graphs of people, researchers must be careful not to harm or expose personal data.

Graph systems can be attacked. Designers must plan for and prevent misuse.
