History of Graph Theory

This timeline shows key moments in the development of graph theory.

1736

Euler and the Seven Bridges

Leonhard Euler solved the puzzle of Königsberg's seven bridges, launching graph theory as a formal field.

1857

Cayley and Trees

Arthur Cayley studied tree structures, linking chemistry and graph theory by counting possible molecular forms.

1878

The Term "Graph"

James Sylvester used "graph" to describe chemical molecules, bridging mathematical concepts with chemistry.

1936

First Textbook

Dénes Kőnig published the first dedicated graph theory textbook, consolidating earlier discoveries.

1969

Harary’s Work

Frank Harary's textbook popularized graph theory in multiple domains, from chemistry to social science.

1976

Four Color Theorem

Proved with computer assistance, it showed any map can be colored with at most four colors, using graph-theoretic ideas.

Late 20th Century

Modern Era

Graph theory expanded into computer science, algorithms, and network analysis, fueling the digital revolution.
