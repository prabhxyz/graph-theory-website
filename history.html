History of Graph Theory

This timeline shows the key moments in the history of graph theory in simple words.

1736

Euler and the Seven Bridges

In 1736, the Swiss mathematician Euler solved the problem of crossing Königsberg’s seven bridges. He showed it was impossible. This work was the first analysis using graphs.

1857

Cayley and Trees

In 1857, Arthur Cayley studied tree structures (graphs with no loops). He also counted trees. His work helped use graphs in chemistry to show how molecules connect.

1878

The Word "Graph"

In 1878, James Sylvester used the word “graph” to compare molecule diagrams with mathematical graphs. This made people see that graphs can be useful outside of pure math.

1936

First Graph Theory Textbook

Dénes Kőnig published the first textbook on graph theory in 1936. This book helped organize many ideas and showed people how to work with graphs.

1969

Harary’s Popular Book

In 1969, Frank Harary wrote a textbook that made graph theory easier for many groups like chemists and engineers. His book helped spread the ideas to many fields.

1976

Four Color Theorem

The Four Color Theorem, which says any map can be colored with four colors, was proved in 1976. It was one of the first big computer-assisted proofs and used ideas from graph theory.

Late 20th Century

The Modern Era

In the late 1900s, graph theory grew to help in computer science, network design, and many other areas. Today, graphs help power the internet, social networks, and many modern technologies.
