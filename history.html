History of Graph Theory

This page tells the story of graph theory in simple words.

In 1736, Euler solved a puzzle about crossing seven bridges. He showed that you cannot cross each bridge only once. This was the start of graph theory.

In 1857, Cayley studied trees (graphs with no loops) and counted them. His work helped use graphs in chemistry to show how molecules are built.

In the 1900s, graph theory grew a lot. Many books were written and new ideas came out. Graph theory helped many fields such as computer science and social studies.
