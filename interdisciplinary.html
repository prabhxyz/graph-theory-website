Graph Theory in Many Fields

Graph theory is not only for computer science. It is used in many subjects. Here are some examples:

  • Mathematics: Studying numbers and shapes with graphs.
  • Physics: Using graphs to understand big systems like power grids.
  • Chemistry: Drawing molecules as graphs to see how atoms join.
  • Biology: Making graphs to show how proteins and genes work together.
  • Social Sciences: Studying friendships and groups with graphs.
  • Linguistics: Looking at words and sentences as networks.
  • Psychology & Neuroscience: Mapping brain connections.
  • Geography & Environment: Mapping routes, rivers, and animal paths.
  • Healthcare: Tracking how diseases spread or how hospitals connect.
  • Arts & Humanities: Showing links between artists, books, or historical figures.
