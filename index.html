Graph Theory Basics

Graph theory is a branch of math and computer science that studies graphs: sets of vertices (dots) connected by edges (lines). It helps us model and understand relationships between objects in many real-world systems.

What is a Graph?

A graph is a structure made of dots (called vertices) and lines (called edges). Edges can be one-way or two-way, and they might have weights (numbers) that represent distances or costs.

Basic Terms

  • Vertex (Node): A dot in the graph.
  • Edge (Link): A line connecting two dots.
  • Path: A route from one dot to another by following edges.
  • Connected Graph: A graph where every dot can reach every other dot.
  • Tree: A connected graph with no loops.
  • Cycle: A loop that starts and ends at the same dot without repeating edges.
