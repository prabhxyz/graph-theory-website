Graph Theory Basics

Graph theory is a part of math and computer science that studies graphs. A graph shows how things are connected. It has dots (called vertices) and lines (called edges) that join the dots.

What is a Graph?

A graph is a simple picture that shows connections. Each dot represents an item, and each line shows a relationship. Lines can be one-way or two-way and may have weights (numbers that show strength).

Basic Terms

  • Vertex (Node): A dot in the graph.
  • Edge (Link): A line connecting two dots.
  • Path: A way to move from one dot to another by following lines.
  • Connected Graph: A graph where every dot can be reached from any other.
  • Tree: A connected graph with no loops.
  • Cycle: A loop that starts and ends at the same dot.
